How to Use This Database

There are two main ways to use the database

1. Filter Search

To find the resources you are looking for, you can check all the categories and filters we created to find specific resouces.
Below are some examples:

  • If you want resources on funding for students, you can check "Funding" in the category "Topic of Resource" and "Students" in the
    category "Target Audience".
  • If you want to know resources for legal help regarding trademarks that are free of charge; you would check the "Legal assistance:
    Intellectual property, Trade marks, Patents" in the "Legal Assistance" category of "Topic of Resouces", as well as "Free" in the
    category "Free or Paid".

To learn more about each category and options, you can scroll down to our explanations of the categories at "Categorization
of the Resources Info" or click here for a pamphlet.

2. Curated Questions Catalog

The curated questions catalog provides a list of commonly asked questions (filters) related to a topic.
This is a great way to learn more if you don't have a specific question or resource needed in mind.

Stage of Business

The stage that a business is in.

Ideation - Entrepreneur has an idea, is trying to implement
Seeding - Business started, selling a bit, still some trial and error in what to do
Establishing - Business started, selling, reached owners expected income - could be income enough for them to sustain themselves or what
they were expecting to generate as a side job
Growing - Business is established and generates income for entrepreneur to sustain themselves, entrepreneur is trying to grow their business
Selling/Exiting - Business owner decided either to close the business or sell
